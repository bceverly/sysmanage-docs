Why Enterprise SaaS

Isolation you can prove

Every tenant lives in its own PostgreSQL database. There is no shared table, no tenant_id column to forget, and no connection from one tenant to another. When a customer asks “can anyone else see our data?”, the answer is a boundary you can point at, not a policy you ask them to trust. See the security treatment.

Credentials that expire themselves

Each tenant's database credentials are leased on demand from OpenBAO — short-lived, auto-expiring, and scoped to one tenant. There is no shared master password to leak, and a captured credential is narrow and brief by construction.

Data residency, per tenant

Because a tenant is a single self-contained database, you can place it where it must live — a specific region or jurisdiction — and back it up, export it, or delete it completely without ever touching another tenant. Right-to-be-forgotten becomes a per-database operation.

Self-service onboarding

Provision a new tenant from the control-plane UI in minutes. A least-privilege provisioner creates the tenant's database and registers it — no manual cluster surgery, and the server never holds superuser rights.

One pane, many fleets

Administer every tenant from a single control plane. Operators switch tenant context to work inside a fleet; server-wide jobs fan out across all tenants. Identity is shared; data never is.

The whole Enterprise toolkit

Enterprise SaaS includes the full Professional+ engine set — compliance, vulnerability scanning, reporting, automation, secrets, and more — running per tenant. Nothing is given up for the multi-tenant model.

Who it is for

  • Managed service providers (MSPs) managing the fleets of many separate customers, who need airtight isolation and per-customer billing, backup, and offboarding.
  • Platform and SaaS companies offering infrastructure management to their own users as a hosted product.
  • Large enterprises and holding companies with independent divisions, subsidiaries, or jurisdictions that must not share a data store.
  • Regulated organizations that need per-tenant data residency, independent backups, and clean, provable deletion.
